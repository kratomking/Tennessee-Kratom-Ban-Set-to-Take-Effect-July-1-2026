Tennessee Kratom Ban Set to Take Effect July 1, 2026, as State Moves to Criminalize Possession and Sales

NASHVILLE, Tenn., May 5, 2026 — Tennessee is preparing to become one of the few states to fully ban kratom after lawmakers approved legislation that will make the possession, sale, manufacturing, and distribution of kratom products illegal statewide beginning July 1, 2026. 

The measure, widely referred to as “[Matthew Davenport’s Law](https://www.billtrack50.com/billdetail/1934245),” passed through the Tennessee General Assembly following months of public debate over kratom safety, addiction concerns, and the rapid growth of concentrated 7-hydroxymitragynine products.

The new law targets all kratom products, including powders, capsules, extracts, liquid shots, and products containing 7-hydroxymitragynine (7-OH), one of kratom’s most potent alkaloids.

State lawmakers said the bill was introduced in response to growing public health concerns surrounding highly concentrated kratom extracts, which are now widely sold in smoke shops, vape stores, convenience stores, and online.

“This law marks a major shift in Tennessee’s approach to kratom and related alkaloid products,” said Alphan Maina, Kratom Experts associate. “For thousands of Tennessee residents who currently use kratom for pain relief, energy, mood support, or opioid withdrawal, this deadline changes everything.”

Under the legislation, individuals found knowingly possessing kratom after July 1 could face criminal penalties, while businesses involved in manufacturing or selling kratom products may face much more serious felony charges under Tennessee law.

The Tennessee ban arrives at a time when state and federal regulators are paying closer attention to concentrated kratom derivatives, especially products marketed as stronger alternatives to traditional kratom leaf products.
Industry groups, treatment providers, retailers, and kratom consumers are now urging current users to understand the law, review available options, and avoid waiting until enforcement begins.
